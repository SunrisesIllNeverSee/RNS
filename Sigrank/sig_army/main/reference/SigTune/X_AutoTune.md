## What Auto-Tuning Really Is

In music: Auto-Tune detects the intended pitch of a voice/instrument, compares it to the actual pitch, and shifts the sound into alignment.
- Can be subtle (correction, stability) or extreme (robotic effects)

Two main ingredients:
- **Reference (Key/Scale)** = the latent X in your equation
- **Tolerance (how strict the correction is)** = your σ band

## Engineering Insights to Pull from Auto-Tune

### 1. Dynamic Correction, Not Flatlining
Good auto-tune doesn't erase nuance — it bends toward stability without flattening vibrato or human edge. MOS²ES needs the same: stabilize threads but keep density and edge intact.

**The trick:** Attack & release times (speed of correction). Too fast = robotic. Too slow = drift.

### 2. User-Selectable Modes
Auto-Tune has modes: transparent correction vs artistic effect.

MOS²ES mirror:
- **Safe-mode correction** (subtle)
- **High-density edge** (intentionally risky, jagged, sharp)

### 3. Context Awareness
Auto-Tune only works if you tell it the key/scale. Otherwise it can't align.

MOS²ES needs its own "key": Is this thread aiming for stability (low drift) or innovation (high tolerance)?

### 4. Creative Exploitation of Imperfection
Artists like T-Pain & Kanye turned Auto-Tune's "flaw" (robotic flattening) into a new artform. Likewise, MOS²ES could exploit collapse-edge behaviors (loops, density spikes) as artistic features when controlled.
