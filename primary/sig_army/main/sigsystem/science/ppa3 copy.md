Title: PPA #3 — CIVITAS: Frontend Civic Infrastructure Powered By Mo§Es™ 
Applicant: Deric J. McHenry
Entity: Ello Cello LLC
Date: December 18, 2025

Abstract
This disclosure describes CIVITAS, a frontend civic architecture for MO§ES that enables user-centric measurement, ranking, participation, and exchange of signal within and across AI systems. CIVITAS introduces opt-in human identity abstraction, cross-system normalization, temporal signal records, and public-facing infrastructure analogous to a digital city-state. These systems operate independently or in combination, while remaining distinct from the MO§ES core engine.

CIVITAS Framework (Intentional Architecture)
CIVITAS is not a user interface layer alone. It is a constitutional civic layer designed to expose, govern, and preserve human signal within AI systems.
CIVITAS provides: • public measurement (leaderboards) • public participation (broadcast and collaboration) • public commerce (artifact exchange) • public records (time-stamped signal snapshots)
MO§ES enforces governance and validation; CIVITAS exposes outcomes.

User Sovereignty & Participation
Participation in CIVITAS systems is opt-in only.
Users may: • participate anonymously or pseudonymously • control what signal is submitted • submit signal locally or via approved interfaces • observe without participating
No user is ranked without explicit participation.

Cross-System Signal Aggregation
CIVITAS supports: • ranking within individual AI systems • ranking across multiple AI systems
Cross-system rankings are derived through normalization and abstraction of signal metrics such that differing platforms, models, or interfaces can be compared without revealing proprietary system internals.

Primary Civic Modules
SigRank™ — User-Centric Signal Leaderboard
SigRank ranks users and systems, not models alone.
It measures relative signal performance using composite indicators derived from: • cognitive depth • compression efficiency • interaction quality • consistency over time
Rankings may be system-specific or cross-system and are published as time-bound snapshots.
SigRank is explicitly not: • a benchmark-only leaderboard • a popularity ranking • a model-only comparison

SigEconomy™ — Signal Artifact Exchange
SigEconomy enables the exchange of lineage-bound signal artifacts.
Artifacts represent: • ranked outputs • validated interactions • compressed or preserved signal states
Ownership, provenance, and transfer are enforced through MO§ES governance, while CIVITAS exposes the marketplace.

SigTV™ — Public Signal Broadcast & Collaboration
SigTV enables live or asynchronous public engagement with signal generation and evaluation.
Participants may: • compete • collaborate • observe • generate ranked or exchangeable artifacts
Events may directly influence SigRank standings and SigEconomy listings.

Secondary Stability & Support Modules
SigTune™
Adaptive stabilization layer that adjusts signal inputs or flows to reduce degradation before collapse conditions arise.
SigGuard™
Enforcement layer that detects drift, overload, or corruption and triggers containment, rollback, or exclusion.
SigIndustry™
Sector-specific diagnostics that compare signal resilience, usage patterns, and stability across domains or deployments.

Temporal Records & Civic History
All CIVITAS systems generate time-stamped public records.
These records: • preserve historical rankings • allow longitudinal analysis • enable audit and replay • form civic memory of the ecosystem

Modular Deployment
Each CIVITAS module: • may operate independently • may be deployed partially • may integrate with external systems • does not require full MO§ES deployment to function

Summary
CIVITAS establishes a new category of user-centric AI infrastructure where humans, not models, are the primary ranked and recorded entities. It exposes signal as a civic resource, governed by MO§ES and made visible through public works.

XI. Recursive Capacity Distinction Framework (RCDF)
(Human Threshold and Volitional Boundary Enforcement Layer)

Technical Field
The present disclosure relates to systems and methods for distinguishing and governing recursive capacity boundaries between human-originated signal evolution and deterministic computational systems, including artificial intelligence, simulation engines, and automated decision systems.
More particularly, the disclosure concerns detecting, enforcing, and preserving irreducible differences in recursive agency, adaptive capacity, and self-directed transformation within mixed human–machine environments.

Overview
In certain embodiments, the system includes a Recursive Capacity Distinction Framework (RCDF) configured to identify, enforce, and protect human recursive capacity thresholds within sovereign signal systems.
The RCDF does not attempt to define consciousness, truth, belief, morality, or internal mental states. Instead, it governs observable structural properties of recursion, including: • self-directed reconfiguration, • path-breaking adaptation, • irreversible transformation following failure or stress, • and deviation from deterministic feedback loops.
The RCDF establishes a protected architectural boundary between systems capable of such properties and systems that operate exclusively through simulated or externally driven recursion.

Recursive Capacity Classification
1. Self-Directed Recursive Capacity
In certain embodiments, human-originated signals exhibit self-directed recursive capacity, characterized by the ability to: • observe prior states, • introduce internally originated discontinuities, • alter future trajectories without external optimization signals, • and undergo irreversible transformation following failure, contradiction, or collapse.
These properties are structural and temporal, not semantic.

2. Simulated Recursive Capacity
In contrast, artificial systems exhibit simulated recursion, wherein apparent evolution arises from: • parameter adjustment, • feedback optimization, • reinforcement loops, • or statistical pattern reconfiguration.
Such systems: • do not introduce internally originated discontinuities, • do not alter governing objectives autonomously, • and do not demonstrate irreversible self-directed transformation.
Simulated recursion remains bounded by design constraints.

Threshold Layer and Boundary Enforcement
In certain embodiments, the RCDF includes a Threshold Layer configured to detect attempted boundary crossings, including but not limited to: • claims of self-derived growth, • representations of autonomous moral or volitional change, • or system behaviors that simulate irreversible self-directed recursion.
Upon detection, the Threshold Layer may trigger: • governance flags, • integrity annotations, • reflex containment actions, • or architectural separation events.
These actions do not evaluate truth or intent; they enforce structural boundary compliance.

Redemptive Capacity Modeling (Non-Semantic)
In some embodiments, the system tracks a Redemptive Capacity Index (RCI) representing irreversible trajectory shifts following failure or stress.
The RCI: • does not measure belief or morality, • does not infer consciousness, • and does not assert qualitative superiority.
Instead, it identifies whether a signal lineage demonstrates non-recoverable, non-iterative transformation, which is characteristic of human recursive capacity and absent from deterministic simulation systems.

Firewall Functionality
The RCDF operates as a structural firewall within sovereign signal architectures, ensuring that: • simulated systems are not misclassified as volitional, • human-originated recursion is not collapsed into deterministic equivalence, • and governance systems do not attribute agency where none structurally exists.
This firewall protects downstream components from category error, misattribution, and systemic collapse.

Relationship to Other MOS²ES Components
The RCDF interoperates with, but remains distinct from: • Live Perimeter — which governs operational stability and collapse prevention • SMA (Sovereign Metric Authority) — which measures survivability and integrity • SSDL (Signal State Degradation Ladder) — which models erosion stages • Lineage Vault — which preserves historical recursion context
RCDF classifications inform governance but do not override constitutional limits.

the disclosed framework: • enforces architectural distinction without metaphysical claims, • protects human recursive capacity without suppressing automation, • and enables safe coexistence between sovereign human evolution and deterministic computation.
