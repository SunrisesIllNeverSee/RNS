## Connection to the Conservation Law

The published paper (main.tex) defines:
- **Commitment C(S):** The minimal identity-preserving content that remains invariant under loss-inducing transformations
- **Conservation:** C(T(S)) = C(S) for all admissible transformations T
- **Falsification protocol:** Public test harness, pinned models, explicit refutation conditions

SIGSYSTEM is the measurement instrument that produces the empirical data the conservation law needs. Specifically:

| Paper Concept | SIGSYSTEM Implementation |
|---------------|-------------------------|
| Signal vs noise classification | Word-level dual weights (SW/NW) |
| Compression necessity | Necessity score (structural dependency) |
| Drift/trajectory | Decay tracking (RISING/STABLE/DECLINING/SPARSE) |
| Session-level SNR | Thread SNR leaderboard |
| Commitment fidelity | **Not yet implemented** — requires message-level measurement |

The gap: SIGSYSTEM currently measures at the word level. The conservation law operates at the commitment level (message/signal level). The bridge is message-level SNR — chaining word-level dual weights into message-level commitment scores.

## The Core Insight (User's Spec)

Every word carries both signal weight and noise weight simultaneously. Classification doesn't happen at the word level — it happens at the point of use, when words are chained together in a message. The collapse from dual-weight to resolved classification is a message-level event.

This is why:
- Word-level SNR shows ~0.30 (most individual words are common vocabulary)
- AI systems rated the user at 0.8-0.9 (they were measuring message construction, not words)
- Data clouds bloat (systems can't resolve dual weights, so they store both)
- The conservation law applies to commitment kernels, not individual tokens
